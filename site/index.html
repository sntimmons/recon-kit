The problem

Every HRIS migration has the same breaking point.

Between export and import, something always breaks. By the time anyone notices, payroll has already run.

📅

Hire dates reset to system defaults

Batches of employees show 2026-02-01 as their hire date. The new system accepted it. Nobody noticed until HR started getting anniversary-day questions for 400 people on the same date.

📈

Salary data gets lost in translation

Currency fields truncate. Payrate columns don't map cleanly. A zero appears where $97,500 should be. The system accepted it, and so did the first payroll run.

⚠

Nobody catches it until it's too late

The standard VLOOKUP spot-check on 200 rows misses the 14,000-row discrepancy hiding in plain sight. By the time payroll flags it, you're already in correction territory.

How it works

Three steps. Full picture.

A tiered identity-resolution engine matches every employee across your legacy and target systems, then audits every matched pair for field-level discrepancies. The whole run takes seconds. The audit trail lasts forever.

Read the full walkthrough →
01

Bring Your Data

Export a CSV from your legacy system, your new system, or both. No reformatting required. No schema changes. The mapping layer handles column variations automatically. Works with Workday, ADP, SuccessFactors, Oracle, and most standard HRIS exports.

02

Run the Engine

Data Whisperer runs tiered identity resolution across your files, matches every employee with a confidence score, enforces strict 1-to-1 pairing, and flags anything it is not certain about for human review. No guessing. No black box. Every match has a reason you can trace.

03

Review and Act

Your results come back as a structured audit report, a mismatch review queue sorted by priority, and exportable correction files for fields that are safe to fix. If the sanity gate detects systemic corruption it blocks corrections automatically until a human signs off.
